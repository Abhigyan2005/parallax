The Last Ride

The wind howled through the empty plains, carrying dust and echoes of the past. The setting sun bled into the horizon, casting long, dying shadows over the land. Cade Harlow rode slow, hunched forward in the saddle, his grip on the reins weak.
His hand pressed against his side,
warm with blood.
A bullet had torn through him hours ago.
Not in a duel, not in some grand act of fate—
he had been betrayed.
He chuckled bitterly, though the sound barely left his lips.
Dyin’ like a fool.
His old partner, Clay, had done this. They were supposed to split the bounty, ride off clean. Instead, Jack had found himself on the wrong end of a barrel. But Clay was not just a partner.
